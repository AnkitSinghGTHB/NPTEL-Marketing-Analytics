--- WEEK 2 ---
* Q1: To identify what consumers want by breaking options into combinations of multiple attributes. The lecture explicitly states, 'The major goal is to identify what consumers want as usual? And we actually what we do  is? We break options available into a combination of multiple attributes.'
* Q2: Utility in the monetary term minus Price. The lecturer defines net consumer surplus as 'Utility in the monetary term minus price'.
* Q3: Increasing value increases cost, which may lead to higher prices that reduce demand, impacting the profit  function. The lecture explains: 'If I increase value, then cost will go up, if I increase cost, the price will go up, if I increase price the  demand will come down.' This describes the dilemma.
--- WEEK 3 ---
* Q1: A The lecturer states that it becomes difficult for a company to cater to many people at once, thus requiring segmentation  to create manageable groups.
* Q2: B Segmentation aims to group consumers such that people within the same group are similar to each other, and people in  different groups are very different from each other.
* Q3: C The 'lukewarm tea' example illustrates that a 'middle path' or compromise offering often fails because it doesn't satisfy  the distinct preferences of any specific segment, leading to zero adoption.
